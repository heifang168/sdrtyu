1000kW Gas Generator Set RFQ Checklist: What Buyers Should Prepare Before Asking for a Quote

A 1000kW gas generator set is usually not a casual purchase. It may be part of an industrial facility, EPC package, factory backup system or distributed power project. A short message asking “price for 1000kW generator” is rarely enough for an accurate quotation.

Introduction

A useful RFQ gives the supplier enough information to check configuration, fuel condition, control requirements, shipment scope and installation preparation. It also helps the buyer compare suppliers more fairly. Without the same technical inputs, quotations may look different because they are not quoting the same scope.

This checklist explains what overseas buyers should prepare before requesting a 1000kW gas generator set quote. It is practical for importers, EPC companies, contractors and industrial users.

The Direct Answer

To quote a 1000kW gas generator set properly, a supplier should know the destination country, fuel type and gas condition, voltage, frequency, running mode, application, quantity, enclosure requirement, site environment and delivery expectations. If possible, the buyer should also share gas composition, gas pressure and expected operating hours.

Confirm The Power Requirement

The first question is whether 1000kW is the required output, the load demand, or a rounded project estimate. These are different. If the buyer has motor starting loads, pumps, compressors or other heavy equipment, the supplier may need to consider starting current and load steps.

Buyers should explain whether they need standby, prime or continuous power. They should also state if multiple units will operate in parallel. For EPC projects, integration with existing electrical systems may affect control panel and synchronization requirements.

Describe The Gas Fuel Clearly

Gas generator quotation depends strongly on fuel condition. Natural gas, biogas, landfill gas and associated gas may have different composition and treatment requirements. Gas pressure, methane content, impurities and stability affect engine selection and performance.

If the buyer cannot provide a full gas analysis at the first inquiry stage, they should at least share the fuel type, source, pressure range and whether the gas supply is stable. The supplier can then request additional details before final confirmation.

Share Electrical Requirements

Voltage and frequency are essential. A generator for 400V 50Hz is not the same as one for 480V 60Hz. The buyer should also mention phase requirements, control system needs, ATS, synchronization, remote monitoring and any local compliance requirements.

If the project will connect to a larger system, the supplier should understand the interface. For example, the generator may need to work with switchgear, transformers, control cabinets or a building management system.

Factory Testing and Pre-Shipment Checks

For a 1000kW project, factory testing should be discussed early. Buyers may ask for inspection photos, control panel checks, wiring checks, basic test records and packaging photos. Load testing scope should be confirmed based on factory capability and project agreement.

Shipment inspection is not only about the generator itself. It also covers accessories, documents, spare parts, packaging method, container loading and identification marks. A clear checklist reduces disputes and makes receiving inspection easier.

Installation Preparation Before Delivery

The supplier can provide better guidance if the buyer shares installation conditions: indoor or outdoor location, ventilation, exhaust route, foundation, ambient temperature, altitude, noise limitations and maintenance access. Gas generator installation also needs attention to gas pipeline safety, pressure regulation and ventilation.

Many problems happen when equipment is selected without installation context. A good RFQ helps both sides identify risks before production and shipping.

FAQ

Can I get a quote without gas analysis?

You can get a preliminary quote, but final configuration should be confirmed after gas condition is reviewed. For serious projects, gas composition and pressure are important.

Is 1000kW suitable for EPC projects?

Yes, but EPC projects often require more scope details, such as synchronization, documentation, packaging, delivery schedule and interface requirements.

What documents should I request before shipment?

Depending on the order scope, buyers can request inspection photos, packing list, basic test records, product photos, nameplate photos and loading photos.

Conclusion

A 1000kW gas generator quotation should be based on project facts, not only capacity. The better the RFQ, the more useful the supplier response will be.

Buyers who prepare fuel, electrical, application and delivery information can reduce revision cycles and compare suppliers more accurately.
